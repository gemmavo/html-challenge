Cloudiness

Based on the Latitude VS. cloudiness scatter plot, there appears to be no correlation between latitude and cloudiness; however, it is notiaceable that there are clumps of 0% as well as 90% but not clear correlation.

It is known that continents tend to be less cloudy than adjacent oceans at the same latitude with the exception of the equatorial belt. On average, cloudiness is highest around 62'S (about 79% of the time) but there is only 40% cloud cover at the South Pole.
